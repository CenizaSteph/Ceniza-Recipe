Lumpia is a popular Filipino dish that consists of thin wrappers filled with a mixture of chopped vegetables, meat, and sometimes seafood, that are then fried until crispy. The filling can vary depending on the recipe, but usually includes carrots, cabbage, green beans, and onions, as well as ground pork or beef.

To make lumpia, the filling ingredients are first sautéed in oil until cooked through, then wrapped in a thin spring roll wrapper made from flour and water. The wrapper is then folded and rolled into a thin, cigar-shaped roll, and secured with a bit of egg wash. The lumpia rolls are then deep-fried until golden brown and crispy.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 lbs ground pork
  • 2 pieces onion, minced
  • 2 pieces carrots, minced
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/2 cup parsley, chopped
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 eggs
  • 50 pieces lumpia wrapper
  • 3 cups cooking oil
