Pork Adobo

Adobo is a popular dish in Filipino cuisine that features meat or vegetables cooked in a tangy and savory sauce made from vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and other spices. The dish is typically made with chicken or pork, but can also be made with seafood, vegetables, or other types of meat.

To make adobo, the meat is first marinated in a mixture of vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns for several hours or overnight. The marinated meat is then browned in a pot or pan before the marinade is added and the dish is simmered until the meat is tender and the sauce has thickened.

Procedures / Tutorials

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork belly
  • 2 tablespoons garlic minced or crushed
  • 5 dried bay leaves
  • 4 tablespoons vinegar
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon peppercorn
  • 2 cups water
  • Salt to taste
