Lechon

Lechon is a traditional Filipino dish that consists of a whole roasted pig, typically stuffed with herbs and spices, and cooked over an open flame until the skin is crispy and the meat is juicy and tender. The dish is often served during special occasions and celebrations, such as weddings, fiestas, and holidays.

To make lechon, a whole pig is first cleaned and gutted before being stuffed with a mixture of herbs and spices, such as lemongrass, garlic, onions, and bay leaves. The pig is then skewered onto a large spit and slowly roasted over an open flame, usually for several hours, until the skin is crispy and the meat is cooked to perfection.
