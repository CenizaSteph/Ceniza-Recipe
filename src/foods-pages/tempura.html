Tempura

Tempura is a Japanese dish consisting of seafood, vegetables, or other ingredients that are battered and deep-fried until crispy. The batter used for tempura is typically made from a mixture of flour, water, and eggs, and is often kept chilled to ensure a light and crispy texture when fried.

Tempura can be made with a wide range of ingredients, but some of the most common include shrimp, squid, scallops, fish, sweet potato, eggplant, mushrooms, and bell peppers. The ingredients are usually cut into bite-sized pieces and then coated in the batter before being deep-fried in oil.

Ingredients

  • 500g Medium-sized tail-on shrimps, peeled and thawed
  • Dry Breading (1 cup Flour)
  • 1 cup Flour
  • 1 cup Cornstarch
  • 1/8 tsp (2 pinches) Hondashi
  • 2 Egg Yolks
  • 2 1/2 cup Ice Cold Water
  • 2 tbsp additional Ice Cold Water
  • Enough oil for deep-frying
  • Optional: Eggplants to complement the ebi/shrimps
