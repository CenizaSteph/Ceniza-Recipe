Sushi

Sushi is a popular Japanese dish that consists of seasoned rice served with a variety of toppings, which may include raw or cooked seafood, vegetables, and egg. The rice used in sushi is typically short-grain rice that is mixed with a seasoned vinegar mixture, known as sushi vinegar, which gives it a slightly tangy taste.

The toppings for sushi can vary widely and may include raw fish such as tuna, salmon, or eel, as well as cooked seafood such as shrimp or octopus. Other popular toppings include avocado, cucumber, pickled daikon, and tamagoyaki (a Japanese-style omelet). The sushi is usually prepared by rolling the toppings and rice together in a sheet of nori (dried seaweed) and then cutting it into bite-sized pieces.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups (400 g) of sushi rice
  • 3 cups (710 mL) of cold water
  • 0.25 cups (59 mL) of rice vinegar
  • ¼ cup (25 g) of granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp (5.7 g) of salt
  • 1 pack of unseasoned nori (seaweed sheets)
  • Sliced vegetables
  • Sliced fish or seafood
  • Pickled ginger (optional)
  • Wasabi (optional)
